Report Deforestation Activities

Install the app onto your mobile devices. The app allows you to report deforestation activities directly to local authorities. Simply take a picture of the deforestation site, and the app will automatically tag the location using your device's GPS. You can then send this report to your local LC5 representative.

The app also enables local authorities to communicate with the community. For instance, your local LC1 can send messages and updates regarding ongoing environmental protection efforts.

All submitted reports are anonymized to protect your privacy. Only the essential information such as the image, location, and description of the deforestation activity are shared with the authorities.

Once the information is entered, submit the report. Your report is then pulled together to include the location (based on the GPS location of your mobile device), date, time, and description (if provided). This data is then reviewed by local authorities for appropriate action.

Visualize Reports

At the same time your report is added to the public interactive map visualization. An icon shows the location of the reported deforestation activity. Your location is obscured on the map by slightly shifting the coordinates to protect your privacy. Tapping a report icon on the map will show the details of the report, including the date, time, and description (if provided). No personally identifiable information is shown on the map.

In addition, the map allows you to view other reports and actions taken by local authorities. This helps keep the community informed and engaged in protecting the environment.

The bottom of the visualization features a timeline where you can select different dates to explore and compare. The color coding indicates the severity of the deforestation activities reported.
